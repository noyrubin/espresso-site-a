How Much Caffeine Is in Espresso? (Per Shot, Double Shot & vs Coffee)

Exactly how much caffeine is in a shot of espresso — single, double, and triple. Plus how espresso caffeine compares to drip coffee, and what actually affects the numbers.

A single shot of espresso contains approximately 60–75mg of caffeine. A double shot — the standard in most café drinks — is roughly 120–150mg.

That’s the quick answer. But the full picture is more nuanced, and understanding it helps you make better decisions about your espresso habit.

Caffeine Per Espresso Shot: The Numbers#

Here’s a reference table covering common espresso types and their typical caffeine content:

Shot TypeVolumeCaffeine (typical)Caffeine (range)
Single shot (solo)1 oz / 30ml~65mg55–80mg
Double shot (doppio)2 oz / 60ml~130mg110–160mg
Triple shot3 oz / 90ml~195mg165–240mg
Ristretto (shorter)0.75 oz / 22ml~50–60mg45–70mg
Lungo (longer)1.5–2 oz / 50–60ml~75–85mg65–100mg

A few important notes on this table:

  • “Double” is the default in most coffee shops. When you order a latte, flat white, or cappuccino, you’re almost certainly getting two shots of espresso (a doppio) as the base.
  • Ristrettos are shorter extractions with less water, so they’re more concentrated but don’t necessarily extract more caffeine per gram of coffee. They often end up with slightly less total caffeine than a standard shot.
  • Lungos pull more water through the same grounds, extracting a bit more caffeine in a larger volume.

Espresso Caffeine vs Coffee: The Comparison That Surprises People#

Espresso is famously “strong” — and by concentration, it is. But “strong” and “high-caffeine” aren’t the same thing.

DrinkVolumeCaffeine
Single espresso1 oz (30ml)60–75mg
Double espresso2 oz (60ml)120–150mg
Drip coffee (8 oz)8 oz (240ml)80–120mg
Drip coffee (12 oz)12 oz (360ml)120–180mg
Cold brew (8 oz)8 oz (240ml)150–200mg
French press (8 oz)8 oz (240ml)80–100mg

Espresso is 6–8× more concentrated per ounce than drip coffee. But you drink 1–2 oz of espresso versus 8–12 oz of drip coffee. That changes the total caffeine calculation significantly.

A single espresso shot (65mg) has less total caffeine than a standard 8 oz cup of drip coffee (90–100mg). A double espresso (130mg) is roughly equivalent to one cup of drip coffee, or slightly more depending on brew strength.

If your goal is maximizing total caffeine intake, a large drip coffee or cold brew actually delivers more caffeine per session than a single espresso.

Is Espresso Stronger Than Coffee?#

Yes and no — it depends how you define “stronger.”

Espresso is stronger by concentration (caffeine per ounce). One ounce of espresso has 60–75mg of caffeine. One ounce of drip coffee has roughly 10–15mg. Espresso is about 5–6× more concentrated. (For why espresso is so concentrated — pressure, ratio, and time — see what is espresso.)

Drip coffee can deliver more total caffeine per serving because you drink a much larger volume. A 12 oz drip coffee (120–160mg) contains more total caffeine than a single or double espresso shot.

This is the key distinction that trips people up: espresso feels more intense because it hits you fast in a small concentrated dose, not necessarily because it has more caffeine per drink.

Think of it this way:

  • Espresso: Fast, concentrated, fewer total milligrams
  • Drip coffee: Slower absorption, larger volume, potentially more total milligrams

What Actually Affects Espresso Caffeine Content#

The 60–75mg per shot figure is an average, but your actual espresso can vary significantly depending on these factors:

1. Dose (Biggest Variable)#

The amount of ground coffee you use is the single largest controllable factor. The standard specialty coffee dose is 18–20g for a double shot. Increasing to 22g or 24g meaningfully increases caffeine:

Dose (double shot)Estimated caffeine
16g~110mg
18g~125mg
20g~140mg
22g~155mg

If you want more caffeine per shot, the most reliable method is simply using more coffee. See our espresso ratio guide for how dose and yield interact.

2. Roast Level#

Counter to what most people expect, lighter roasts have slightly more caffeine than darker roasts. Caffeine is heat-sensitive, and the roasting process breaks down a small percentage of caffeine molecules over time.

The difference is modest — roughly 5–10% more caffeine in a light roast vs a dark roast at the same dose. This won’t be noticeable in normal use.

The darker roast myth probably persists because dark roast espresso tastes more intense and bitter, which people associate with higher caffeine. But intensity of flavor and caffeine content are separate things.

3. Bean Species (Arabica vs Robusta)#

Robusta coffee beans contain roughly twice the caffeine of Arabica beans (approximately 2.7% caffeine vs 1.5% by weight on average).

Most specialty espresso uses 100% Arabica. Some blends — particularly classic Italian espresso blends — include a percentage of Robusta for crema, body, and caffeine kick. If you’re drinking a Robusta-heavy blend, your shot likely contains more caffeine than an all-Arabica equivalent.

4. Extraction Time and Yield#

A longer extraction pulls more caffeine from the grounds. A standard espresso extraction (25–30 seconds, 2:1 or 2.5:1 yield) extracts perhaps 90–95% of available caffeine.

Ristrettos (shorter, less water) extract slightly less caffeine despite being more concentrated per ounce. Lungos (more water) extract a bit more.

5. Water Temperature#

Standard espresso extraction targets 90–96°C (195–205°F). Higher temperatures extract slightly more compounds from the coffee, including caffeine. The variance here is small in normal home brewing.

Brand-Specific Caffeine Data#

If you’re using commercial espresso products, here are reliable estimates:

Starbucks#

  • Single espresso shot: ~75mg
  • Double shot: ~150mg
  • Most Starbucks drinks (Latte, Cappuccino, Flat White) = 2 shots = ~150mg
  • Tall latte (1 shot): ~75mg
  • Grande/Venti latte (2–3 shots): ~150–225mg

Nespresso Original Line#

Most capsules produce 60–65mg per 40ml shot. Intensity ratings on the packaging refer to roast level and flavor intensity, not caffeine. A “10 Intensity” pod doesn’t have more caffeine than a “5 Intensity” pod — they may have nearly the same amount.

Nespresso Vertuo Line#

Varies by capsule size:

  • Espresso (40ml): ~60–65mg
  • Double Espresso (80ml): ~120–130mg
  • Gran Lungo (150ml): ~120–200mg
  • Mug (230ml): ~130–175mg
  • Alto (414ml): ~175–200mg

Pods and Capsules (General)#

Single-serve pods and capsules typically use 5–7g of ground coffee per shot, compared to 18–20g in specialty home brewing. This means most single-serve espresso pods deliver less caffeine than home-pulled specialty espresso — often in the 50–65mg range.

Daily Caffeine Limits: How Many Shots Is Too Many?#

The FDA considers up to 400mg of caffeine per day safe for most healthy adults. That’s roughly:

  • 5–6 single espresso shots
  • 2–3 double espresso shots
  • About 4 standard cups of drip coffee

Sensitivity varies significantly by individual, body weight, and tolerance. If you’re experiencing jitteriness, poor sleep, or anxiety, reducing caffeine is worth experimenting with regardless of where you fall relative to the general guidelines.

Pregnant women are generally advised to limit caffeine to 200mg/day or less.

Decaf Espresso: Not Zero Caffeine#

Decaffeinated espresso still contains a small amount of caffeine — typically 2–7mg per shot. Decaffeination removes 97–99.9% of caffeine, not all of it.

If you’re sensitive to caffeine or avoiding it for medical reasons, decaf espresso is substantially lower but not caffeine-free. For more on the four decaffeination methods, espresso dial-in tips for decaf beans, and how to pick a quality decaf, see our full guide on what is decaf coffee.

Quick Reference: Espresso Caffeine Facts#

  • Single shot: ~60–75mg caffeine
  • Double shot: ~120–150mg caffeine
  • Espresso is more concentrated than drip coffee per ounce (6–8× stronger)
  • Per drink, drip coffee often has equal or more total caffeine than a single espresso
  • Lighter roasts have marginally more caffeine than darker roasts
  • Dose (coffee weight) is your biggest variable — more grounds = more caffeine
  • Decaf espresso still has 2–7mg of caffeine per shot
